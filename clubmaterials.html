Club Materials

Climbing a mountain requires high-quality materials.


  • Marketing
  • Lesson Planning and Team Management

To make your club popular, you must market it to both potential students and potential volunteers.

Potential Students: Make a flyer that gives details about what your club offers compared to other clubs, and details for your first meeting. Such details include:

  1. Students will learn programming logic and UI development
  2. Students will be taught how to make their own apps, and will develop a portfolio of projects on GitHub for later use for internship applications and hackathons
  3. Time and date of club meeting
  4. Place of club meeting
  5. Email for contact

Post these flyers around the campus, or on community notice boards, depending on what students you would like your club to have.

(for school clubs: another great source for advertisement is a school’s video news bulletin. If you have a quick 30s segment that captures the above details, you’re gold :D)

Potential Volunteers: Make a flyer that gives details about what your club offers compared to other clubs, and details for your first meeting. Such details include:

  1. Learning through teaching: learn new development skills, and put them into practice by teaching them to another group
  2. Community service: Get volunteer hours while still doing work in technology
  3. Build a portfolio of projects for use in future internships and opportunities
  4. Time and date of first club meeting
  5. Place of club meeting
  6. Email for contact

Post these flyers around the campus, or on community notice boards, depending on which community you’d like to reach out to for volunteering.

(for school clubs: another great placement for advertisement is a school’s video news bulletin. If you have a quick 30s segment that captures the above details, you’re gold :D)

-Lessons should be done a week in advance of teaching a group to understand what is to be covered

-While all the lessons are online, it is a good idea to explain certain topics in greater depth, such as the different variable types, loops, and data structures. Evaluate with your volunteer team what is right for you

-As a founder, try and make each volunteer have an equal impact in the club. If you always find that you are speaking in front of the students, try and get another volunteer to learn from you and speak. This lessens your burden and also helps the volunteer enrich their coding knowledge.
